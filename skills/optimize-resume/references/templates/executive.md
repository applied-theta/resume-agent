# Executive Resume Template

## Key Differences from Standard Template

### Executive Summary vs Professional Summary

Executive summaries are longer (3-5 sentences vs 2-4) and emphasize:
- Scale of leadership (team sizes, budget, geographic scope)
- Business-level outcomes (revenue, market share, valuation)
- Strategic vision and transformation capability
- Industry authority and thought leadership

### Core Competencies vs Technical Skills

Executives list competencies rather than technical skills:
- Focus on leadership capabilities, not individual contributor skills
- Include business functions (P&L, M&A, Board Relations)
- Include domain expertise rather than specific tools
- Keep technical skills high-level (platform categories, not individual languages)

### Experience Section Enhancements

- **Scope line:** Each role includes a scope summary (team size, budget, geography)
- **Company context:** Brief context for companies that are not widely known
- **Business metrics:** Every bullet includes business-level impact (revenue, cost savings, market share)
- **Earlier Career section:** Roles older than 15 years condensed to title/company/dates

### Additional Sections

- **Board & Advisory Roles:** Demonstrates industry influence and governance experience
- **Speaking & Publications:** Establishes thought leadership and industry presence

---

## Guidance by Experience Level

### VP / SVP (10-15 Years Total, 3-5 in Executive Roles)

- 2-3 detailed experience entries plus Earlier Career section
- Focus on team scaling, process transformation, and measurable business impact
- Core competencies should bridge technical depth and leadership breadth
- Length: 2 pages

### C-Suite / CEO / CTO / CPO (15+ Years Total, 5+ in Executive Roles)

- 2-4 detailed experience entries plus Earlier Career section
- Focus on company-level impact: revenue, fundraising, market position, organizational transformation
- Include board memberships and advisory roles prominently
- Speaking engagements and publications reinforce authority
- Length: 2 pages (3 pages only if extensive board/publication record warrants it)

---

## What to Emphasize

- **Business outcomes over technical details:** "Drove $12M annual revenue growth" not "Implemented new CRM system"
- **Organizational impact:** Team growth, culture initiatives, retention improvements
- **Strategic decisions:** Technology bets, build-vs-buy decisions, platform migrations with business justification
- **Cross-functional leadership:** Collaboration with C-suite peers, board communication, customer-facing activities
- **Scale indicators:** Budget size, team size, geographic scope, transaction volume

## What to De-Emphasize

- **Individual contributor work:** Unless it directly demonstrates strategic thinking
- **Specific technologies:** List at the platform/category level, not individual tools
- **Day-to-day management tasks:** Focus on transformational achievements, not operational duties
- **Early career details:** Condense roles older than 15 years
- **Education details:** Degree and institution only; no GPA, no coursework (unless Ivy League or top MBA is a differentiator)

---

## Formatting Guidelines

- **Font:** Conservative, professional (Garamond, Calibri, Times New Roman)
- **Margins:** 0.75-1 inch on all sides
- **Font size:** 11-12pt for body text, 14-16pt for name
- **Tone:** Authoritative, strategic, forward-looking
- **Numbers:** Use specific figures whenever possible; large numbers in readable format ($12M, 200+ engineers)
- **Date format:** "Month YYYY" consistently throughout
- **No tables, text boxes, columns, or images** -- ATS compatibility required
- **Length:** 2 pages standard; 3 pages only with extensive board/publication record
