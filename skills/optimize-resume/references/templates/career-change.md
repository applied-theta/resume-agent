# Career Change Resume Template

This template is used for the `career_changer` and `military_transitioner` archetypes -- candidates transitioning from one industry or career path to another. The key challenge is reframing previous experience as relevant to the target field.

## Key Differences from Standard Template

### Two Experience Sections

Career change resumes split experience into:
1. **Relevant Experience:** New-field roles (internships, bootcamp projects, freelance, volunteer work in target industry)
2. **Previous Experience:** Prior career roles, reframed for transferable skills

This structure puts target-relevant experience first, even if it is more recent and less substantial.

### Skills Section Reframing

Skills are organized by relevance to the target career:
- **Target Industry Skills** first (even if newly learned)
- **Transferable Skills** second (communication, project management, data analysis, etc.)
- **Tools & Technologies** third

### Summary as a Bridge

The professional summary is the most critical section for career changers. It must:
1. State the target role identity (not the previous career identity)
2. Frame previous experience as an asset, not a liability
3. Demonstrate commitment through recent training or projects
4. Articulate the unique value the candidate's background provides

### Education & Training Prominence

Recent education, bootcamps, and certifications are elevated above previous experience because they demonstrate current capability and commitment to the transition.

## Reframing Previous Experience

The key skill in career-change resume writing is translating achievements from one domain into language that resonates in the target domain.

### Teaching to Tech

| Previous (Teaching) | Reframed (Tech) |
|--------------------|-----------------|
| "Managed a classroom of 30 students" | "Managed concurrent workstreams for 30 stakeholders with competing priorities" |
| "Developed curriculum for AP Computer Science" | "Designed and delivered technical curriculum covering data structures, algorithms, and software development fundamentals" |
| "Assessed student performance using data-driven methods" | "Analyzed performance metrics across [X] data points to identify trends and optimize outcomes" |

### Military to Civilian

| Previous (Military) | Reframed (Civilian) |
|---------------------|---------------------|
| "Led a platoon of 40 soldiers" | "Led cross-functional team of 40 members in high-stakes operational environments" |
| "Managed $5M in equipment and assets" | "Managed $5M equipment portfolio including procurement, maintenance, and lifecycle planning" |
| "Conducted intelligence briefings for senior officers" | "Delivered executive briefings synthesizing complex data into actionable strategic recommendations" |

### Finance to Tech

| Previous (Finance) | Reframed (Tech) |
|--------------------|-----------------|
| "Built financial models for investment analysis" | "Built data models and automated analysis pipelines processing [X] data points for decision support" |
| "Managed client portfolio worth $50M" | "Managed complex stakeholder relationships with $50M in business impact" |

### General Reframing Rules

1. **Replace jargon:** Translate industry-specific terms into general business or target-industry language
2. **Highlight transferable skills:** Project management, data analysis, stakeholder communication, problem-solving, mentoring
3. **Quantify impact:** Numbers transfer across industries -- $X saved, X people managed, X% improvement
4. **Focus on outcomes, not duties:** What you achieved matters more than what your job description said
5. **Remove irrelevant details:** Previous career bullets should be 2-3 maximum, focused only on transferable value

## Guidance by Transition Stage

### Early Transition (Learning Phase)

- Just started bootcamp/degree, no target-industry experience yet
- **Emphasis:** Education/training, personal projects, previous career's transferable skills
- **Projects section:** Critical -- these are the primary evidence of new capability
- **Previous experience:** Reframe 2-3 most relevant roles
- **Length:** 1 page

### Mid-Transition (Building Credibility)

- Completed training, working on projects, possibly freelancing or volunteering
- **Emphasis:** Projects, freelance/volunteer work, training credentials
- **Relevant Experience:** Include any volunteer, freelance, or contract work in target field
- **Previous experience:** Condensed to transferable highlights
- **Length:** 1-2 pages

### Late Transition (Landed First Role)

- Has 1+ roles in target industry
- **Emphasis:** Target-industry roles (even if junior)
- **Previous experience:** Brief, transferable-only, may be moved to "Earlier Career" summary
- **Consider switching to standard template** once target-industry experience is substantial
- **Length:** 1-2 pages

---

## What to Emphasize

- **Target role identity:** Lead with who you are becoming, not who you were
- **Transferable achievements:** Reframe previous accomplishments for the new context
- **Recent training and credentials:** Show investment in the transition
- **Projects in the new field:** Demonstrate hands-on capability
- **Unique perspective:** Your previous career gives you insights others lack
- **Learning velocity:** Show rapid skill acquisition and adaptability

## What to De-Emphasize

- **Previous career title prominence:** Do not lead with a title that signals the wrong field
- **Industry-specific jargon:** Translate into universal business language
- **Duties from previous career:** Only include if they demonstrate transferable skills
- **Employment gaps during transition:** Frame as intentional career pivot with training
- **Unrelated certifications or skills:** Remove previous-career credentials that do not transfer

---

## Formatting Guidelines

- **Font:** Clean, modern (Calibri, Arial)
- **Margins:** 0.5-1 inch on all sides
- **Font size:** 10-12pt for body text
- **Section headers:** Make "Relevant Experience" and "Education & Training" visually prominent
- **Summary:** Slightly larger or bolder to draw the reader's eye -- this is the most important section
- **No tables, text boxes, columns, or images** -- ATS compatibility required
- **Length:** 1-2 pages; err on the side of conciseness
- **Date format:** "Month YYYY" consistently throughout
